## CONCERNS & MITIGATION

### **⚠️ Regulatory Risk: Gamification of Investing**

**The problem:**
- SEC/FINRA have cracked down on "gamification" (see: Robinhood confetti controversy)
- They worry it encourages reckless trading

**Our mitigation:**
1. **Competition is on risk-adjusted returns, not pure returns**
   - Leaderboard shows Sharpe ratio, not just % gain
   - Penalizes excessive risk-taking
   - Rewards smart, diversified strategies

2. **Amanda provides guardrails**
   - "You're about to make a very risky trade. Sure?"
   - "Your correlation just spiked to 0.9. Want to diversify first?"
   - Educational prompts BEFORE risky actions

3. **Transparency with regulators**
   - Proactively share approach with SEC
   - Position as "educational + competitive" not "gambling"
   - Compare to sports leagues (fantasy football isn't gambling)

4. **No loot boxes, no random rewards**
   - Everything is based on actual performance
   - No slot machine mechanics
   - No "spin to win" bullshit

5. **Opt-in, not default**
   - Users choose to join leagues
   - Can use platform without social features
   - Personal space (Amanda) is primary interface

**Messaging:**
"We're making investing social and educational, not turning it into a game. Competition drives learning, and our AI ensures users don't take reckless risks."

---

### **⚠️ Social Pressure to Trade**

**The problem:**
- Users might feel pressured to trade just to keep up in leagues
- Overtrading is bad for returns

**Mitigation:**
1. **Leaderboards reward Sharpe ratio, not trade volume**
   - Fewer, better trades beat frequent mediocre trades
   - "Quality over quantity" messaging

2. **Amanda discourages overtrading**
   - "You've made 15 trades this week. Studies show frequent trading reduces returns. Want to slow down?"

3. **"Buy and hold" leagues**
   - Competition for best long-term strategy
   - Minimum hold period (30 days, 90 days, 1 year)
   - Rewards patience

4. **Penalties for excessive risk**
   - Leaderboard shows risk-adjusted returns
   - Taking 10x risk for 2x return doesn't win leagues

---

### **⚠️ Privacy / Bragging Creates Bad Behavior**

**The problem:**
- Users might share misleading info (cherry-pick wins, hide losses)
- "Influencer trading" can lead followers astray

**Mitigation:**
1. **Verified performance**
   - Shared screenshots are authenticated (can't fake)
   - Shows all-time performance, not just cherry-picked trades

2. **Community moderation**
   - Report button for misleading info
   - Top contributors have reputation scores
   - Bad actors get flagged/banned

3. **Amanda fact-checks in communities**
   - User posts: "NVDA going to $1000!"
   - Amanda can comment: "Historical analysis suggests this valuation would require..."
   - Keeps discussion grounded
